# Day 4 – Hour 1: SIEM Fundamentals

## What is a SIEM?

SIEM stands for Security Information and Event Management.

A SIEM collects, stores, analyzes, and correlates logs from multiple systems to detect suspicious activity and generate alerts.

## Key Functions

- Log Collection
- Log Normalization
- Correlation
- Alerting
- Investigation Support

## Alert Workflow

Event → Log → SIEM → Correlation Rule → Alert → Analyst Investigation

## Why Organizations Use SIEMs

- Centralized logging
- Threat detection
- Faster investigations
- Compliance
- Incident response

## Key Insight

A SIEM automates the collection and correlation of logs, allowing SOC analysts to focus on investigating alerts rather than manually reviewing thousands of events.
